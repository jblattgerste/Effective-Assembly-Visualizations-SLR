# Effective Step-by-Step Assembly Visualizations SLR

This repository provides the supplementary dataset and static browsing page for the paper:

> Jonas Blattgerste and Thies Pfeiffer. 2026. What Constitutes Effective Step-by-Step Assembly Visualizations? A Systematic Scoping Review and Call for More Empirical Research. In Proceedings of the 19th International Conference on PErvasive Technologies Related to Assistive Environments.

[![Project Web Page](https://img.shields.io/badge/Project-Web%20Page-1f6feb?style=for-the-badge)](https://jblattgerste.github.io/Effective-Assembly-Visualizations-SLR/)
[![Citation Metadata](https://img.shields.io/badge/Citation-CFF%20Metadata-0a7a5a?style=for-the-badge)](https://github.com/jblattgerste/Effective-Assembly-Visualizations-SLR/blob/main/CITATION.cff)

The dataset documents the initial publication database collected during the systematic scoping review. It contains the 68 publications identified after two bidirectional snowballing iterations, before the paper's additional empirical inclusion criterion was applied.

The final paper reports a corpus of 26 empirical studies after scope refinement and additional snowballing. The broader 68-publication database is published here for transparency and to support researchers working on visual assembly instructions.

## Dataset Scope

The initial dataset includes peer-reviewed English-language publications published before 2026 that explicitly investigate, propose, discuss, or evaluate visual design parameters contributing to the effectiveness of step-by-step assembly instruction steps for adults and non-immersive media.

The website and CSV intentionally preserve this broader pre-refinement set, including publications that were later excluded from the final empirical corpus.
